Bio

I’m a designer, an artist, a writer and even a bit of a re-searcher.

I guess you could say I am an experimentalist at heart. There is nothing I love more than working at the intersections of fields, disciplines, knowledge systems, and methodologies.

I have a Bachelors of Environmental Design from the University of British Columbia. My degree allowed me to delve into the intersections of built and natural environment, studying architecture and landscape architecture, but also ecology, conservation and science.

I am currently living as a 2.5 generational chinese settler on the unceded ancestral lands of the hən̓q̓əmin̓əm̓ and Sḵwx̱wú7mesh sníchim-speaking peoples (xʷməθkʷəy̓əm, Sḵwx̱wú7mesh, and səl̓ilw̓ətaʔɬ), also known as Vancouver, Canada.

A fun question and a fun fact

Why is your username abcsouper?

When I was in Singapore, I stayed with my friend’s cousins. Their grandma (ahma) would come over and make us food. One day she served a soup with carrots, large pieces of corn cobs and pork. This was abc soup! (Name after the vitamins the soup supposedly provides)

Meanwhile in Asia, I was also reflecting on my lived experiences of being born and raised in America and Canada. living in Asia, I became hyper aware of how I really was a hybrid, third-culture kid of sorts. It was kind of confusing, kind of mixed up - like soup!

Before meeting this soup, I associated abc with the alphabet, and the acronym - american born chinese (abc). Like me. So abc soup spoke to me in this moment.

And I became abcsouper.
